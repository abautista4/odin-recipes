Chana Masala (butchered)

One of the first vegetarian recipes I learned was chana masala. In fact, Indian/Southern Asian food in general is absoluetely a God-send for vegetarians and vegans! Chana masala in particular is my favorite. Chickpeas are pretty high in protein and fiber, so they make you feel more satisfied compared to other vegetarian foods in my opinion. I've been trying to increase my tolerance to spicy foods, but Indian spicy is a whole 'nother level, so I modified it a bit (hence ‘butchered’). This also follows Adam Ragusea's recipe, which can be found here! Again, I made my own changes to it. Hope you enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of canned chickpeas/garbanzo beanas
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion
  • A few cloves of garlic
  • 1 can of chiles jalape√±os
  • 1 tbsp of garam masala
  • 1 tbsp of turmeric
  • 1 tbsp of cumin
  • 1 tbsp of Kashmiri chili powder
  • a handful of Fennugreek leaves (Keshuri methi)
  • 1 tsp of garlic powder
  • Salt
  • Coconut oil
  • Vinegar/lemon juice
  • Fresh cilantro
  • 1 tsp of Better than Boullion - No Chicken base

Recipe:

  1. Heat 1 - 2 tbsps of coconut oil in a pot over medium heat. Wait for it to shimmer.
  2. Add onions, saute until soft and dark.
  3. Stir in chiles, tomatoes, chickpeas, garlic powder, onion power, dried ginger. Saute for around 2 - 3 minutes.
  4. Add garam masala, turmeric, cumin, and chili powder. Fry until fragrant - around 30 seconds
  5. Add enough water to cover everything, around 1 - 1 1/2 cups, or 1 can. Add soup base.
  6. Simmer for around 30 - 45 minutes, or until sauce thickens into a gravy-like consistency
  7. Serve over rice of your choice.
